Transposition Cipher

The Transposition Cipher encrypts by rearranging the positions of the characters in the plaintext based on a key. The key defines the rearrangement pattern.

Example:

Plaintext: HELLO

Key (3 columns):

H E L
L O .
. . .

Ciphertext: HOELL

Explanation: The characters are rearranged in columns based on the key.
