Atbash Cipher

The Atbash Cipher is a simple substitution cipher where the alphabet is reversed. A becomes Z, B becomes Y, and so on. It’s a special case of the Caesar Cipher with a shift of 13 (for the English alphabet).

Example:

Plaintext: HELLO

Atbash Cipher: SVOOL

Explanation: Each letter in the plaintext is mapped to its reverse counterpart in the alphabet: H -> S, E -> V, L -> O, and O -> L.
